Data Concepts Learned

Correlation vs. Causation

expand_more

Definition:

Understanding that just because two variables move together (correlation) does not mean one causes the other (causation).

Case Example:

The rise in ice cream sales correlated with the suspect's activity, but the heatwave was the causal factor for both, not the ice cream.

Outlier Detection

expand_more

Definition:

Identifying data points that deviate significantly from other observations, which may indicate variability, errors, or novelties.

Case Example:

A single, unusually large financial transaction was an outlier that led to uncovering the entire money laundering scheme.
